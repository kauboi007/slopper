# dctect - DCT Based AI Image Detector

This project is not vibecoded.

## What It Does

Detects whether an image is AI-generated by analyzing its frequency domain fingerprint using the Discrete Cosine Transform. No pretrained models. No ML libraries. Pure signal processing math implemented from scratch.

## How It Works

Real photographs follow the 1/f^2 power law. Energy in the frequency domain drops off predictably as frequency increases. AI-generated images violate this law in measurable ways because the upsampling operations inside diffusion models introduce periodic artifacts that show up as anomalies in the frequency spectrum.

The detector computes four features from the DCT power spectrum and combines them into a single score from 0 to 100. Higher score means more likely real. Below 50 means likely AI.

**Spectral Slope** measures how steeply energy drops off with frequency. Real images have slopes around -3.5 to -4.5. AI images tend toward flatter slopes.

**High Frequency Ratio** measures what fraction of total energy lives in high frequency components. AI images leak more energy into high frequencies than real photos.

**Spectral Flatness** measures how evenly energy is distributed across the high frequency zone. AI upsampling creates unnaturally uniform high frequency energy. This was the most reliable feature in testing.

**Spike Score** measures periodic artifacts in the frequency profile using autocorrelation. Diffusion models upsample at fixed scale factors which creates regular spikes that do not appear in real photos.

## Implementation

DCT is implemented from the formula using two for loops. No scipy, no cv2, no sklearn.

```
X[k] = a(k) * sum(x[n] * cos(pi * (2n+1) * k / 2N))
```

2D DCT is applied by running 1D DCT on every row, then every column of the result.

The power spectrum is computed by squaring DCT coefficients and averaging across all 8x8 blocks of the image.

## Results

Tested on personal photos and images from Midjourney, DALL-E, and Stable Diffusion. Correctly classified standard AI generators. Struggled with photorealistic models like Nanobanana  which are specifically trained to match natural image statistics. This is an open problem in the field.

| Image | HFR | Slope | Flatness | Spike | Score | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|Real 1 | 0.174 | -3.48 | 0.40 | 0.39 | 55.2 | REAL |
| Real 2 | 0.076 | -4.19 | 0.37 | 0.31 | 61.5 | REAL |
| Midjourney image | 0.083 | -3.04 | 0.73 | 0.31 | 35.8 | AI |
| Nanobanana image | 0.058 | -4.82 | 0.23 | 0.21 | 77.3 | WRONG |

## Limitations

Photorealistic AI models that are specifically trained on large datasets of real photographs can match natural frequency statistics closely enough to fool this detector. Detection accuracy degrades as generative models improve. This is expected and documented.

The detector works on grayscale intensity only. Color channel analysis and cross-channel correlation are left as future work.

## What I Learned

DCT is a change of basis. You are projecting pixel values onto a set of orthogonal cosine basis vectors and measuring how much of each frequency is present. The (2n+1) offset in the formula shifts sampling to pixel centers to avoid discontinuities at block boundaries. 2D DCT is separable so you apply 1D DCT twice. The power spectrum obeys the 1/f^2 law for natural images and deviations from this law are measurable signals.
